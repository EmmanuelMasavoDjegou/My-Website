Research & Publications

Research Focus: Statistical Methods for Survival Data Modeling in the Era of AI

My research spans three complementary directions: semiparametric effective age models for recurrent events, deep learning integration with survival analysis, and high-dimensional variable selection for meta-analysis.

1

Efficient Estimation in Semiparametric Accelerated Gap-Time Models for Recurrent Event Data

The Canadian Journal of Statistics
Semiparametric Models Survival Analysis Recurrent Events

Develops efficient semiparametric methods for accelerated gap-time models with effective age processes, expanding classical survival analysis to handle recurrent events with complex intervention mechanisms.

View Details →
2

RNN-AGT: A Recurrent Neural Network Framework for Accelerated Gap-Time Models via Gehan-Type Rank Loss

Statistics in Medicine
Deep Learning Survival Analysis Neural Networks

Introduces a deep learning framework that relaxes parametric assumptions in gap-time models using RNNs and novel Gehan-type rank loss, enabling flexible prediction of recurrent event timing.

View Details →
3

Hierarchical Variable Selection for Nonconvex Penalized Cox Models in IPD Meta-Analysis

Statistical Methods in Medical Research
Meta-Analysis Variable Selection High-Dimensional Data

Develops hierarchical penalized Cox models for individual-participant-data meta-analysis, addressing between-study heterogeneity and enabling stable variable selection in high-dimensional genomic survival data.
